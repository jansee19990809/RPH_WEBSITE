Learnings and Insights of the PH Constitution Evolution

  • Regaspe, Emerson Felipe & Salvador, Vince Angel
  • December 14, 2020

Constitution is a written instrument embodying the rules of a political or social organization. The basic principles and laws of a nation, state, or social group that determine the powers and duties of the government and guarantee certain rights to the people in it is called Constitution. This only discuss the changes of two constitution and these are 1899 and 1935 Constitutions

The 1899 Constitution is also known as the Malolos Constitution. This has been the declaration chartered by several members of the Katipunan in hopes of making the Philippines an independent nation. It has been ratified or made valid by the Katipuneros and tried to rally international countries for support. This constitution, as history recounts, was not recognized internationally. Inside this Constitution is the Preamble were created for every Filipinos freedom to vote, choose and command their own Filipino people. It was created to beg for their own freedom in their own land. Under the Constitution Preamble are the Constitution articles that has fourteen titles and this constitution has Transitory Provision where it details the gradual transition from the Spanish constitution to this written constitution, which also details emergency instructions during wartime, as the government established was a REVOLUTIONARY government, which is aimed for the benefit of soldiers and able men and women who can be of service in times of war.

The 1935 Constitution also known as the Commonwealth Constitution, this has been the Constitution made by the Filipinos but ratified by both Filipinos and Americans. It introduced the Commission on Elections (COMELEC)as well as the recognition that the Philippines will be put under US rule. It was made after it was amended using the following Acts passed by the US Government in preparation for government independence: The Philippine Organic Act (1902), Jones Law (1916), Hare-Hawes-Cutting Act (1933), Tydings-McDuffie Act (1934). Inside this Constitution is its Preamble where it was created for every Filipino to remember and cherish our freedom, and secure to the next generation our justice and democracy. This constitution has eighteen articles and has an ORDINANCE APPENDED TO THE 1935 CONSTITUTION where it detailed the overall control of the United States over the Philippines due to the agreement made by the two nations, including the usage of every natural resources of the country and the free establishment of foreign businesses in the country.

The Constitutional changes in both of this constitution is that Constitution in 1899 focuses on Philippine sovereignty and the revolution against Spain so that it was created for the freedom of the Filipinos in its own land while Constitution in 1935 was written for the Commonwealth government wherein Filipinos can have seats of power while under American policies and created for every Filipino to remember and cherish our freedom, and secure to the next generation our justice and democracy.

I conclude that every constitution is important because it protects our rights. It enables us to have justice and live a beautiful life. It helps us to know our limitations and the right things we should do in order for us to become a good citizen. It ensures that those who make decisions on behalf of the public fairly represent public opinion. It also sets out the ways in which those who exercise power may be held accountable to the people they serve. We will know how to protect ourselves from enemies.
