Constitution: Learning Advantage for Students

  • Reyes, Mc Jefferson & Reyes, Michael James
  • Decenber 14, 2020

Every country has their own constitution; however, it is not the same for all. In the Philippines, we have a set of rules or laws that each of us must obey. It is important to know the constitution of the country that we are living in, because these laws not only protect the one’s that made it and the ones that practice the law, but also us, the citizens. Knowing it has its own set of advantages. One of it is being protected. If we know our rights, no one can use our weaknesses to ourselves, no one can oppress us, not even the government officials. By knowing the constitution, we would know what is right to do and what is not. We would not do anything that we know is not legal, and in times of needs, we can use these laws to our own protection. For example, when we are being held as a suspect, if we know our rights, they can’t use it against us, and the officials can’t imprison us without us knowing what we did wrong. We can also protect our family members when they are being held liable for something. And if all of the people know their constitution, we can’t be used as a pawn to pay the wrong things that we didn’t do.
