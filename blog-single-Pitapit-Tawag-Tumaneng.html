Philippine Constitution: From a Learner's Point of View

  • Tawag, Karl Ian
  • Tumaneng, Leobert
  • Pitapit, Joshua
  • December 14, 2020
Learning Constitution

During our time from grade school to highschool, we've been taught that the independence we have now is because of our national heroes. How Rizal inspired everyone to have a revolution and how Bonifacio led Filipinos on fighting. But we really have no idea how it was established, how it became official.

Learning how our constitution developed over the years opened my mind and appreciate those people in the background who worked on it. Especially on the 1935 constitution which started and ignited the path of the Philippines on it's independence. I've learned how different our era was to what before, our rights then was pretty lackluster but I do understand it because it was on the verge of World War II.

On my understanding, the basic definition of constitution is it's the law for the government. It makes sure that what the government's doing was according to it's existence. Being knowledgeable about it made me understand how the government should run and making them accountable for their actions.

We are in democratic country, the people have the supreme power. It's important that we realize how significant our constitution is and what's in it. We should know how the country should be ran, how we should act and how we should be treated. We are our this country, not the government.


Advantage of studying Philippine Constitution as a College student

Nowadays even law schools do not even recognize the significance of the Philippine Constitution.It is deeply unfortunate that primary and secondary schools do not use this occasion to improve our youth’s knowledge and comprehension of our Constitution. As a College Student, it is important to know how extremely important the nation’s relationship to its constitution which plays a huge part in its socio-economic development. As for individual matter we must value the constitution because it drives forward to reach the goals of a more oriented rule of laws than one does not aware of.

Indeed, a society that truly believes in constitutional values will be most likely more cohesive than one that does not. Therefore, a nation which treats its constitution both as a foundational document and as an existential guide to political life will have a much better shot at socio-economic progress than one that does not.
