An Overview History of the Philippine Constitution

  • See, Jan Wilbert
  • December 14, 2020

Across our rich and broad history, the best evolution that we had is the continuous formulation and ratification of the country's Constitution. That will be a symbol of our country's image of having an organized and structured government and management of our citizens in our beloved motherland.

From the First Republic (Revolutionary Government) up until the Fifth Republic (People Power Government), we saw how people change and look at our country's pride and fight. And to maintain the continuous democracy and sovereignty of it.



The timeline below shows the short history and description of each of our Philippine Constitution. Which has been formulated, ratified, and amended throughout our Philippine Government Timeline.


First Republic (Revolutionary Government)
1899 Constitution
This Constitution was formulated and ratified during the occupation of the Spaniards here in the Philippines. Approved in the Malolos Congress on November 29, 1898, and drafted by Pres. Emilio Aguinaldo it on December 1, 1898, to create the amendments and was refused by Congress.

This Constitution is also called the Malolos Constitution.



Commonwealth Period
1935 Constitution
Approved by the 1934 Constitutional Convention on February 8, 1935. Certified by the US President on March 25, 1935, and ratified by plebiscite on May 14, 1935.

There is a Constitutional Amendment created on June 18, 1940, and on March 11, 1947.



Second Republic (Japanese Occupation)
1943 Constitution
Approved by the Preparatory Committee on Philippine Independence on September 4, 1943.

And it was ratified by the KALIBAPI Convention, September 7, 1943.



The Third Republic and The Fourth Republic (Martial Law, the New Republic, and Parliamentary Government)
1973 Constitution
Drafted by Pres. Ferdinand Marcos to the 1971 Constitutional Convention on December 1, 1972. Ratified by Citizensâ€™ Assemblies held from January 10 to 15, 1973.

Proclaim forcefully by the President via Proclamation on January 17, 1973.

There is also an amendment on October 16-17, 1976, on January 30, 1980, and April 7, 1981.



Fifth Republic (People Power Constitution)
1986 Constitution
Promulgated by the effect of the Presidential Proclamation by Pres. Corazon Aquino on March 25, 1986.

Also known as the Freedom Constitution.

1987 Constitution
Approved by the 1986 Constitutional Commission on October 12, 1986.

The 1987 Constitution of the Republic of the Philippines was presented to Pres. Corazon Aquino on October 15, 1986.

It was ratified on February 2, 1987, by a plebiscite. It was proclaimed in force on February 11, 1987.
